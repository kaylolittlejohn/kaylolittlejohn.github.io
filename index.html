About

Research centered on expressive, personalized communication.

My research is focused on speech modeling and natural language processing. I’m fortunate to be advised by Gopala K. Anumanchipalli and Edward F. Chang, with support from the NSF Graduate Research Fellowship Program. I am also part of the Chang Lab at UCSF.

I co-led the development of multimodal AI tools that translate brain activity into text, audible personalized speech, and a high-fidelity digital talking avatar, including work published in Nature (2023) and Nature Neuroscience (2025). My current work explores improved methods for naturalistic speech synthesis and spoken language modeling.

I earned my B.S. at Columbia University, where I studied under Paul Sajda.
